	<title>"We couldn't find any data in your Excel workbook"</title>
	
	<h1>Problem</h1><p>When you import an Excel workbook into Power BI, you may see the following error:</p><p><span><img src="https://powerbi.uservoice.com/assets/82713955/PBI_WeCouldntFindAnyData.png"></span><br></p><h1>Quick solution</h1><p>1. Edit your workbook in Excel.<br>2. Select the range of cells that contain your data. The first row should contain your column headers (the column names).<br>3. Press <b>Ctrl + T</b> to create a table.<br>4. Save your workbook.<br>5. Return to Power BI and import your workbook again.</p><h1>Details</h1><h2>Cause</h2><p>In Excel, you can create a <b>table </b>out of a range of cells, which makes it easier to sort, filter, and format data.</p><p>When you import an Excel workbook, Power BI looks for these tables and imports them into a dataset; if it doesn't find any tables, you'll see this error message.</p><h2>Solution</h2><p>1. Open your workbook in Excel. (Note: The pictures here are of Excel 2013. If you're using Excel 2007 or 2010, things may look a little different, but the steps are the same.)</p><p><span><img src="https://powerbi.uservoice.com/assets/82791859/PBI_Trb_XLWksht1.png"></span><br></p><p>2. Select the range of cells that contain your data. The first row should contain your column headers (the column names):</p><p><span><img src="https://powerbi.uservoice.com/assets/82791871/PBI_Trb_XLWksht2.png"></span><br></p><p>3. In the ribbon on the <b>INSERT </b>tab, click <b>Table</b>. (Or, as a shortcut, press <b>Ctrl + T</b>.)</p><p><span><img src="https://powerbi.uservoice.com/assets/82791883/PBI_Trb_XLWksht3.png"></span><br></p><p>4. You'll see the following dialog. Make sure <b>My table has headers</b>&nbsp;is checked, and select&nbsp;<b>OK</b>:</p><p><span><img src="https://powerbi.uservoice.com/assets/82791901/PBI_Trb_XLCreateTbl.png"></span><br></p><p>5. Now your data is formatted as a table:</p><p><span><img src="https://powerbi.uservoice.com/assets/82791910/PBI_Trb_XLTbl.png"></span><br></p><p>6. Save your workbook.<br>7. Return to Power BI.&nbsp;Select Get Data at the bottom of the left navigation pane.</p><p><span><img src="https://powerbi.uservoice.com/assets/82714033/PBI_GetData.png"></span><br></p><p>8. In the <b>Files </b>box, select <b>Get</b>.</p><p><span><img src="https://powerbi.uservoice.com/assets/82714318/PBI_GetFiles.png"></span><br></p><p>9. Import your Excel workbook again. This time, the import should find the table and succeed.</p><p>If the import still fails, let us know by clicking <b>Contact support </b>in the help menu:</p><p><span><img src="https://powerbi.uservoice.com/assets/79679610/PBI_CldntFindData_ContactSupt.png"></span><br></p><h1>More information</h1><ul><li>This article applies to Excel 2007, 2010, and 2013.</li><li>The pictures here are of Excel 2013. If you're using Excel 2007 or 2010, things may look a little different, but the steps are the same.</li></ul><i>Error: We couldn't find any data in your Excel workbook. Your data might not be formatted properly. You'll need to edit your workbook in Excel and then import it again.</i><br><div></div>
